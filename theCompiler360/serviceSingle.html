End-to-end compliance support so you can focus on growing your business while we manage your compliance needs.

The regulatory landscape in Africa is evolving rapidly. New laws, tightening compliance requirements, and increasing supervisory expectations are reshaping how financial institutions operate. For banks, fintechs, payment providers, lenders, and insurance companies, staying compliant is no longer just about avoiding penalties-it's about building trust, driving growth, and creating sustainable value.

1. A New Era of Regulation in Africa

Across the continent, regulators are strengthening frameworks around consumer protection, data privacy, cybersecurity, capital adequacy, and anti-money laundering, Initiatives by the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), Bank of Ghana, South African Reserve Bank (SARB), and other regulators are raising the bar for compliance.

Institutions must move from a reactive approach to a proactive, risk-based compliance culture.

2. Key Compliance Challenges

  • Keeping up with changing regulations across multiple jurisdictions

  • Managing AML/CFT, KYC, and sanctions compliance effectively

What Are the Key AML requirements for financial Instruction in Africa?

Here are the key AML requirements under Nigerian regulations:

  • Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

  • Customer Identification and Verification (KYC)

  • Ongoing Monitoring of Transactions

  • Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)
